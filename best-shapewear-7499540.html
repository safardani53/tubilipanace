The 16 Best Shapewear of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

When we think of shapewear, we often think of restrictive and uncomfortable undergarments that sculpt the body but also pinch, poke, and, frankly, make us feel a little claustrophobic. With brands like Kim Kardashian’s Skims and, of course, the iconic Spanx creating more innovative designs, shapewear has come a long way and is actually really comfortable (you just have to know where to look) without sacrificing its purpose: to smooth, sculpt, and shape the body. 

“Shapewear is a beneficial item to have in your wardrobe because it can make a huge difference in what your final outfit will look like,” says Cami Raymond, the senior design director at Ruby Ribbon. “It helps the fit and smoothes your curves to give you a more defined waistline and the confidence you deserve,” she adds. Despite its name, shapewear isn’t just for contouring the body, it’s also beneficial for dealing with tricky fabrics and ensuring they drape properly on the body. “There are times with a clingy or very delicate, thin fabric that shapewear can help smooth over the body and add a little extra coverage,” says Alison Bruhn, a personal stylist and co-founder of The Style That Binds Us.

With so many different shapewear styles available, we decided it was high time for us to find our tried and true pieces — so the PEOPLE Tested team put dozens of designs to the real-world test to find the best shapewear pieces across multiple categories including bodysuits, shorts, and waist cinchers. We evaluated each shapewear piece for level of control, target area, fit, comfort, color, and durability.

Things to Consider Before Buying Shapewear

Level of Control 

When shopping for shapewear, Raymond says to think about the type of shapewear and the level of control that you need. “Some types of shapewear will provide a light shaping or smoothing for every day and some shapewear will provide a tighter compression and sculpting for that special occasion dress or outfit,” she explains. For the most part, shapewear brands will note the level of control and sometimes even offer different levels of control for the same silhouette.

Target Area 

Raymond also recommends buying for the targeted area, not just for the way a piece of shapewear looks (although, these two things can go hand-in-hand). “Not all shapewear is created equal, so look for styles that focus on the areas you are looking to shape,” she notes. Shapewear will commonly target the tummy, waist, butt, and hips. However, there are also shapewear pieces that target the thighs, bust, and even the back. 

The good news is that you don’t need to worry about getting too specific and having a piece of shapewear for every target area since many pieces target multiple areas. For example, our top pick for the best bodysuit, the Skims Sculpting Bodysuit With Snaps, is designed to target the tummy, butt, and chest. The Spanx Suit Your Fancy Plunge Low-Back Mid-Thigh Bodysuit targets the thighs, bust, waist, and tummy with a supportive built-in bra, too.

Comfort 

“Just because you are wearing shapewear doesn’t mean it should not be comfortable,” says Raymond. The key to comfortable shapewear is sizing and fit since it’s something you’ll wear for long periods of time and you don’t want to feel too restricted. In addition to sizing, the fabric can also make or break comfort. “Make sure the fabric has breathability as well as wicking properties to make you feel cool and dry if you’ll be wearing shapewear all day,” Raymond adds.

Color 

Since shapewear is technically an undergarment, Raymond says it’s important to consider the color of the shapewear as well as the garment you wear on top. “Keep in mind the color of your outfit and skin tone so it matches and can prevent any see-through issues,” she explains.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What shapewear brand is best?

  • Which shapewear is best for lower belly pooch?

    “If you’re looking for shapewear to smooth out your lower belly area, look for high-waisted bottoms or choose a longer slip-like shapewear or a shaping cami that has a longer length that works on shaping your entire body,” says Raymond. Bruhn also notes that you can purchase tummy-control underwear which can help smooth out the lower belly but, if you have concerns about panty lines and want something a little more seamless, she says to “choose a shapewear option that goes from mid-thigh to waist or even up to the rib cage.” 

    The Spanx Suit Your Fancy Plunge Low-Back Mid-Thigh Bodysuit is a good example of this type of shapewear, as it targets the mid-thigh area as well as the lower body, waist, hips, and even the bust. But if you prefer something that doesn’t cover the chest, you can also try our top pick for the best-targeted compression shapewear, the Honeylove SuperPower Short, which has specific design details to smooth and shape the tummy area.

  • Is it better to size up or down in shapewear?

    For shapewear to be effective, it has to be skintight, or at least close-fitting. However, particularly with higher compression shapewear, you don’t want it to be so tight that it’s uncomfortable. If you’re buying online, check the measurements (if provided by the retailer) to choose the correct size. Otherwise, purchase shapewear in your regular size (not a size down).
